BEACON

Methodology feedback for producers who know what they're chasing.
the gap
You finish a bounce at 3am.
Is it loud enough for a big room? Mono-safe on the subs? Does the spectral balance sit where the label you're aiming at actually lives? You can watch a masterclass ten times and still not know if you applied it right. There's no feedback between the lesson and the session.
the stack
What's actually built.

Local · Python

A parser that reads your Ableton .als project file (gzipped XML) and your bounced .wav. Extracts every track, device chain, plugin parameter, routing, grouping, groove pool setting. Audio analysis via librosa: loudness, 7-band spectrum, stereo field, transient profile, sidechain depth, crest factor.

Output: one structured JSON. What's actually in the session, not what you think is in the session.

AI agent · evaluation

The JSON is evaluated by an AI agent (Claude, Anthropic) loaded with a complete methodology reference — documented signal chains, confirmed plugin parameters, arrangement philosophy, spectral targets.

The agent compares your session state against the methodology and flags divergences: wrong Overdrive frequency, missing bus processing, spectral centroid drifting from the reference profile. Priority-ordered. Specific. Actionable.

Single-variable per bounce. Each change isolated and measurable. Delta tracking across iterations shows whether the move worked — not whether it felt like it worked. The agent doesn't generate music or make creative decisions. It reads your session and tells you where you diverged from the methodology you chose to follow.
